Wetware Upgrade

The ceiling of vibe coding isn’t AI — it’s biology.

Harvard Business Review recently published “When Using AI Leads to Brain Fry”, surveying 1,488 full-time workers and finding that 14% of AI users experience “AI brain fry.” After reading it, I realized — this is exactly what I’ve been going through the past few months. In my last post, I talked about how the bottleneck ultimately lands on the human brain. This is a problem most people will run into eventually.

AI brain fry shows up as mental exhaustion, a buzzing sensation, brain fog, difficulty focusing, slower decision-making, headaches, and the feeling that you need to stop and reset. It’s like having too many browser tabs open in your head — your thinking becomes cluttered. The cause: mainly high-intensity AI supervision — monitoring multiple tool outputs at once.

Vibe coding is a high-cognitive task, so burnout is inevitable. Since it’s cognitive, reset with higher quality sleep and exercise — weight training plus cardio. Daily supplementation helps too: zinc and magnesium aid nerve recovery and sleep, curcumin is anti-inflammatory, fish oil (Omega-3) is essential brain nutrition, and creatine provides a slight energy boost. Safe supplements with solid evidence. I’ve been taking them since my gym days — stable, no issues.

Reduce decision-making during the process. The best case is AI that can one-shot the development. The debugging loop is what really drains cognition. Make AI as capable as possible — whether through better scaffolding or other means — and reduce the number of decisions at the root.

Don’t open too many sessions. I used to run nine terminals — unnecessary. Two to three main development terminals is already enough to keep most people busy. Beyond three you’re just multi-tasking, and humans are bad at that. Especially at night, your brain’s context window is running low — decision quality drops off a cliff. You need sleep to compact. From my own data, coding at night produces significantly more bugs — but mornings don’t. I think some of our cognitive mechanisms are remarkably similar to how LLMs work. Fascinating.

Pushing the ceiling of vibe coding requires biohacking.

湿件升级

Vibe coding 的天花板不是 AI，是生物硬件。

哈佛商业评论最近发了一篇文章叫《When Using AI Leads to “Brain Fry”》，调查了 1,488 个全职员工，发现 14% 的 AI 使用者出现了“AI 脑烧”。我看完之后觉得，这就是我过去几个月一直在经历的东西。上一篇聊到瓶颈最终会落在人脑上，这是未来大部分人都会遇到的问题。

AI 脑烧（brain fry）表现为精神疲惫、嗡嗡感、精神雾、注意力困难、决策变慢、头痛，以及需要暂停工作重置的感觉，像大脑中打开太多浏览器标签导致的杂乱思考。原因主要源于高强度 AI 监督（如监控多个工具输出）。

Vibe coding 是高认知任务，就一定会有 burnout。既然是认知任务，那就用更高质量的睡眠和运动去 reset，包括 weight training 和有氧训练。日常补剂也有必要：锌和镁有助于神经恢复和睡眠，姜黄素抗炎，鱼油（Omega-3）是大脑必需的营养物质，肌酸能略微提升能量水平。都是很安全的补剂，有足够的证据证明有效。我从健身时期就开始用了，一直很稳定。

再就是减少中间过程的决策参与，让 AI 能 one shot 开发是最好的。调试过程很耗认知，最好是把 AI 的能力训练得越强越好，不管是通过脚手架还是其他手段，从根源上减少决策数量。

不要开太多 Session。我之前说一共开九个 Terminal，其实没必要，两到三个主开发的 Terminal 就已经够大部分人“吃一壶”的了。本质上超过三个就是在做 Multi-tasking，而人类很难做到真正的多任务并行。尤其到晚上，人脑的 Context window 已经不够用了，决策质量断崖下降。需要 sleep 来 compact。据我的个人数据观察，晚上处理 coding 问题会导致 bug 明显增加，但早上就不会。我觉得人的有些认知机制和 LLM 其实是高度相似的，非常有意思。

提高 vibe coding 的上限，需要 Biohacking。
