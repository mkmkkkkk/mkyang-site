The Bottleneck Is My Brain

The ceiling of vibe coding isn’t AI — it’s biology.

Harvard Business Review recently published an article called “When Using AI Leads to Brain Fry”, surveying 1,488 full-time workers and finding that 14% of AI users experience “AI brain fry.” After reading it, I realized — this is exactly what I’ve been going through the past few months.

In my last post, I talked about how the bottleneck ultimately lands on the human brain. This post is about what to do about it.

I think this is a problem most people will run into eventually. AI brain fry shows up as mental exhaustion, a buzzing sensation, brain fog, difficulty focusing, slower decision-making, headaches, and the feeling that you need to stop and reset. It’s like having too many browser tabs open in your head — your thinking becomes cluttered.

The cause: mainly high-intensity AI supervision — monitoring multiple tool outputs — leading to a 14% increase in mental effort, 12% in mental fatigue, and 19% in information overload. On top of that, AI amplifies workload, task-switching, and dense interaction.

Here’s my solution.

Vibe coding is a high-cognitive task, so burnout is inevitable. Since it’s cognitive, reset it with higher quality sleep and exercise — weight training plus cardio.

You also need less decision-making during the process. The best case is AI that can one-shot the development. The debugging loop in between is what really drains cognition. The middle steps are never good anyway — the goal is to make AI as capable as possible, whether through better scaffolding or other means. Solve decision fatigue at the root. Reduce the number of decisions.

Don’t open too many sessions. Beyond three, the negative impact on your brain is massive. I used to say I run nine terminals — that’s unnecessary. Two to three main development terminals is already enough to keep most people busy. Opening more seriously tanks your efficiency. You’re essentially multi-tasking, and that’s really hard to do. Especially at night, your brain’s context window is already running low — decision quality drops off a cliff. You need sleep to compact.

I noticed something interesting: when I work on these things at night, bugs are way more frequent. But in the morning, they’re not. Fascinating.

Some daily supplementation is also necessary — creatine, fish oil, magnesium, zinc, and curcumin. These are very safe supplements with solid evidence behind them. I started taking them a long time ago from my gym days, and they’ve been stable. So pushing the ceiling of vibe coding requires biohacking.

瓶颈是我的脑子

Vibe coding 的天花板不是 AI，是生物硬件。

哈佛商业评论最近发了一篇文章叫《When Using AI Leads to “Brain Fry”》，调查了 1,488 个全职员工，发现 14% 的 AI 使用者出现了“AI 脑烧”。我看完之后觉得，这就是我过去几个月一直在经历的东西。

上一篇聊到瓶颈最终会落在人脑上。这篇接着聊——怎么解决。

我觉得这是未来大部分人都会遇到的问题。AI 脑烧（brain fry）表现为精神疲惫、嗡嗡感、精神雾、注意力困难、决策变慢、头痛，以及需要暂停工作重置的感觉，像大脑中打开太多浏览器标签导致的杂乱思考。

原因：主要源于高强度 AI 监督（如监控多个工具输出），导致精神努力增加 14%、精神疲惫 12%、信息过载 19%。此外包括 AI 加剧工作量、多任务切换、密集互动。

我给一个解决方案。

Vibe coding 是高认知任务，就一定会有 burnout。既然是认知任务，那就用更高质量的睡眠和运动去 reset 这种 burnout，包括 weight training 和一些其他的有氧训练。

再就是需要更少的中间过程决策参与，让 AI 能 one shot 开发是最好的。中间调试过程其实很耗认知，反正中间过程都不好，最好是把 AI 的能力训练得越强越好。不管是通过各种脚手架还是其他手段，从根源上解决我的决策疲劳，减少决策数量。

还有就是不要开太多的 Session。超过三个 Session 之后，会对人脑认知产生极大的负面影响。我之前说一共开九个 Terminal，其实没必要。实际上，两到三个主开发的 Terminal 就已经够大部分人“吃一壶”的了。开得太多之后会严重影响效率，本质上是在做 Multi-tasking（多任务并行），而这其实是很难做到的。尤其到晚上，人脑的 Context window 就已经不够用了，决策质量会断崖下降。需要 sleep 来 compact。

所以我发现我晚上处理这些事情的时候，bug 特别多，但是早上就不会，非常有意思。

然后一些日常的补充是有必要的，包括服用肌酸、鱼油、镁、锌还有姜黄素。这些都是很安全的补剂，并且有足够的证据证明它是有效果的。我很早就开始在用了，因为之前 Gym 有用过很长一段时间，都还挺稳定的。所以提高 vibe coding 的上限是需要 Biohacking 的。
